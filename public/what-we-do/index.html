What We Do

The ALUNA Research Fellowship is a selective program that will only be accepting 4-5 students per pathway in the preliminary batch of applications. Accepted students will have the opportunity to work alongside like-minded and equally skilled peers while developing their research projects. All research fellows will be expected to be committed to developing their own AI skills, as well as contributing equally to their research teamâ€™s project.

Note that the ALUNA Research Fellowship is not a class; we expect that you have a strong foundation in programming and AI and that you will be willing to spend your own time to cover material that you are unfamiliar with. We expect a high level of professionalism and responsibility from all of our research fellows, as would be expected in any other research environment. Research fellows may be required to complete certain online certifications or parts of online certifications as part of their background research for their projects. The end goal of each research team will be to publish their research paper in a peer-reviewed journal. This accomplishment is highly valued in the research community and in the process of college admissions. ALUNA research mentors will be guiding research fellows in making sure their work is high quality and relevant to the research being done in bioinformatics
